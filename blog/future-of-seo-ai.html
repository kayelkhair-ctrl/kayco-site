The Future of SEO: How AI Is Changing Search

By Kay & Co. / 11 June 2026 / 6 min read

Every few years someone declares that "SEO is dead." This time, something genuinely big is happening, but it isn't the death of SEO. It's the biggest expansion of search in two decades, driven by AI. Here's where search is heading, and what it means for UK businesses.

Is SEO dead? No, it's expanding

The fundamentals of search engine optimisation, relevance, clear structure, and authority, are not going away. In fact, they now matter more, because the same signals that rank you on Google also feed AI search. What's changing is the surface area: alongside ranked links, there's now a whole new layer of AI Overviews and answer engines to win.

From ten blue links to one synthesised answer

The defining shift is from a list of links to a single answer. When Google shows an AI Overview, or when someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity, a synthesised response often replaces the results page. The metric that matters is no longer just your ranking, it's whether you're cited in the answer. That's the domain of Generative Engine Optimisation.

SEO isn't dying. It's splitting into two complementary disciplines, and the brands that master both will dominate.

Three forces shaping the future of SEO

  • Answer-first search. More queries resolve without a click, raising the value of citations over rankings alone.
  • Entities over keywords. AI systems reason about who and what you are, so clear entity definition becomes central, see LLM SEO.
  • Multi-engine discovery. Visibility now spans Google, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini, making AI visibility the unifying metric.

What won't change

Despite the disruption, the core job stays the same: be genuinely useful, clear, and trustworthy. Technical health, helpful content, and earned authority remain the foundation, AI simply raises the reward for doing them well and the cost of doing them badly.

What UK businesses should do now

  1. Get the fundamentals right, fast, crawlable, well-structured pages (technical SEO).
  2. Define your entity consistently across your site and the web.
  3. Write to be cited, factual, structured, question-led content.
  4. Add structured data, FAQ, Article, Organization, and Speakable schema.
  5. Measure AI visibility and iterate as the landscape evolves.

The takeaway

The future of SEO is not less SEO, it's SEO plus GEO, working as one. The brands that start now build a compounding advantage while competitors wait. Kay & Co. is a UK SEO and GEO consultancy built for exactly this moment.
