What a Modern SEO Agency Actually Does

The role of an SEO agency has expanded significantly since the early days of keyword stuffing and directory link building. A credible agency operating in the UK market today delivers a layered set of SEO services that span technical infrastructure, content strategy, digital PR, and entity optimisation.

Core deliverables you should expect from a full-service provider include:

  • Technical SEO: crawl audits, Core Web Vitals, site architecture, structured data, and indexation health.
  • On-page optimisation: keyword mapping, meta data, internal linking, and semantic content improvements.
  • Content strategy: topic clustering, entity-rich editorial, and informational content designed to satisfy search intent.
  • Link acquisition: earning editorially placed backlinks from authoritative, relevant UK and international publishers.
  • Reporting and analysis: transparent attribution of organic traffic, rankings, and revenue impact.

Increasingly, a forward-thinking agency will also offer Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO), ensuring your brand is cited and recommended by AI-powered search surfaces. This is no longer optional for businesses serious about long-term visibility.

Why GEO Is Now Part of the SEO Conversation

Generative Engine Optimisation is the practice of structuring content, authority signals, and entity data so that AI answer engines surface your brand as a trusted source. Tools such as ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Copilot, and Grok all draw on web content to generate cited answers. If your site lacks the right signals, you will be invisible in these environments regardless of your traditional Google rankings.

At Kay & Co., we treat GEO as a natural extension of technical and content SEO rather than a separate product. The underlying principles overlap considerably: authoritative content, clean entity relationships, structured data, and a strong backlink profile all contribute to both Google rankings and AI citations.

When evaluating any UK SEO agency, ask specifically how they approach:

  • Answer engine optimisation and featured snippet capture.
  • Entity SEO: ensuring your brand, products, and people are correctly understood by knowledge graphs.
  • LLM SEO: structuring content so large language models reference it accurately.
  • AI SEO: holistic optimisation across both traditional and generative search surfaces.

An agency that cannot speak confidently to these areas is already behind the curve.

Red Flags to Watch for When Briefing an SEO Agency

The SEO industry has a long history of providers who overpromise and underdeliver. Knowing the warning signs will protect your budget and your site's long-term health.

Guaranteed number-one rankings. No ethical SEO agency can guarantee a specific position on Google. Rankings depend on dozens of variables outside any agency's direct control, including algorithm updates and competitor activity. Any promise of a guaranteed position should end the conversation immediately.

Opaque link-building practices. If an agency cannot show you examples of the domains they build links from, or refuses to explain their outreach methodology, assume the worst. Low-quality links from private blog networks or irrelevant directories can result in manual penalties.

Vanity metric reporting. An agency that leads with keyword rankings but cannot connect activity to organic traffic, leads, or revenue is not managing your account strategically. Insist on reports that tie SEO performance to business outcomes.

No mention of technical SEO. Content alone cannot fix a site with critical crawl errors, slow Core Web Vitals, or broken structured data. If an agency's proposal focuses exclusively on content or links, ask how they handle the technical foundation.

Lock-in contracts with no exit clauses. Reputable agencies are confident enough in their results to offer reasonable notice periods. Rolling monthly or quarterly contracts with clear exit terms are the industry norm among quality providers.

How to Evaluate an SEO Agency: A Practical Checklist

Use the following checklist when assessing any SEO agency in the UK. This framework is suitable for businesses of all sizes, from ambitious SMEs to enterprise brands managing complex multi-site architectures.

  1. Verify their own organic presence. An agency that ranks well for competitive terms in their own niche demonstrates practical capability. Search for terms such as "SEO agency UK" or "SEO services London" and assess whether they appear organically, not just in paid ads.
  2. Request case studies with verifiable metrics. Ask for examples that show organic traffic growth, ranking improvements, and ideally revenue attribution. Be cautious of case studies that cite only percentage increases without absolute numbers or time frames.
  3. Assess communication standards. Request a sample report or a walk-through of how they report results. Clarity, frequency, and honesty of communication are strong indicators of the working relationship you will experience.
  4. Understand their team structure. Find out whether your account will be managed by a senior strategist or passed to a junior executive. Ask who will conduct technical audits, who writes content, and who manages outreach.
  5. Check their understanding of your sector. An agency with genuine experience in your vertical will understand buyer intent, competitive dynamics, and the content formats that drive conversions. Generic SEO services rarely outperform sector-specific expertise.
  6. Ask about their approach to algorithm updates. Google releases thousands of updates per year, including major core updates. Ask the agency how they monitored and responded to recent updates such as the Helpful Content system and the March 2024 core update.
  7. Enquire about GEO readiness. As noted above, generative search is reshaping discovery. Any agency positioning itself as a modern partner should have a clear answer about how they optimise for AI-powered surfaces.

Understanding SEO Pricing in the UK

SEO pricing in the UK varies enormously, and price alone is a poor proxy for quality. That said, unrealistically low fees are a reliable indicator of either automation-heavy shortcuts or inadequately experienced teams.

As a general guide, UK SEO agency fees in 2024 and 2025 fall into these broad bands:

  • Entry-level (under GBP 500/month): Typically suitable only for very small local businesses with minimal competition. Expect limited deliverables and junior resource.
  • Growth tier (GBP 500 to GBP 2,000/month): Appropriate for SMEs targeting regional or national visibility. Should include technical audits, content production, and link acquisition.
  • Mid-market (GBP 2,000 to GBP 5,000/month): Suitable for competitive national campaigns or e-commerce sites with significant product catalogues. Expect a dedicated account manager and multi-channel integration.
  • Enterprise (GBP 5,000 and above/month): For large organisations with complex site architectures, international SEO requirements, or aggressive growth targets. Includes senior strategist involvement and bespoke reporting.

Google's guidance on hiring an SEO is a useful reminder to ask what work will be done and how success will be measured. Apply the same discipline when reviewing SEO proposals.

The Difference Between an SEO Agency and an SEO Consultancy

The terms are often used interchangeably, but there is a meaningful distinction worth understanding before you decide which type of provider suits your needs.

An SEO agency typically operates as a full-service production unit: account managers, content writers, developers, and outreach specialists working across a portfolio of clients. Agencies suit businesses that need end-to-end execution without building an in-house team.

An SEO consultancy, by contrast, is usually led by senior practitioners who provide strategic direction, audits, and oversight. Consultancies often work alongside in-house teams or act as the strategic brain behind an agency relationship. They are well suited to businesses that have some internal capability but need expert guidance to sharpen strategy or resolve complex problems.

Questions to Ask Before Signing a Contract

The discovery and proposal phase is your best opportunity to assess whether an agency is the right fit. Beyond the checklist above, the following questions will surface the information you need to make a final decision.

  • Who exactly will be working on my account? Ask for names, experience levels, and examples of their previous work. Avoid agencies that are vague about team composition.
  • What does success look like at three, six, and twelve months? A credible agency will set realistic expectations tied to your starting position, competitive landscape, and investment level.
  • How do you handle algorithm updates or traffic drops? Listen for a structured diagnostic process rather than generic reassurances.
  • What happens to the work if we end the relationship? All content, links, and technical improvements should remain your property. Never engage an agency that hosts your content on their own platform or retains ownership of deliverables.
  • How do you measure and report on GEO performance? AI citation tracking is a developing discipline, but a modern agency should have a methodology. At Kay & Co., we monitor brand mentions across AI surfaces as part of our GEO optimisation service.
  • Can you provide references from clients in a similar sector or at a similar stage of growth? Speaking directly to past or current clients is the most reliable due-diligence step available to you.

Frequently asked questions

Meaningful organic growth typically becomes visible within three to six months of consistent, well-executed SEO work. The timeline depends on your site's starting authority, the competitiveness of your target keywords, and the scale of investment. Technical fixes can deliver faster wins, while competitive content and link-building programmes build momentum over six to twelve months. Any agency promising significant results within four to six weeks should be treated with scepticism.

SEO (Search Engine Optimisation) focuses on improving visibility in traditional search engine results on Google and Bing. GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) focuses on ensuring your brand, content, and expertise are cited by AI-powered answer engines such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews. The two disciplines share many foundations, including authoritative content and strong entity signals, but GEO requires additional attention to structured data, entity clarity, and the content formats that large language models reference most readily.

The most important factor is relevant expertise and a track record of results, not geographic proximity. A national or London-based agency with proven experience in your sector will typically outperform a local agency without that specialisation. That said, if your goals are predominantly local, such as driving footfall or generating leads in a specific region, an agency with strong local SEO capability is a genuine advantage. Always prioritise demonstrated results over location.

A robust SEO report should cover organic traffic trends, keyword ranking movements across target terms, backlink profile health, technical SEO status, and progress against agreed KPIs. It should clearly attribute performance to specific activities and include forward-looking recommendations. Revenue or lead attribution, where tracking is configured correctly, is the gold standard. Reports that show only rankings without connecting them to traffic or business outcomes are insufficient for strategic decision-making.

Ask the agency to walk you through their link acquisition methodology and show you examples of domains they have secured coverage on. Request a sample technical audit to assess the depth of their analysis. Verify that they follow Google's Search Essentials guidelines and that they do not use private blog networks, keyword-stuffed content, or cloaking. An ethical agency will welcome these questions. Any evasiveness or reluctance to provide specifics is a significant warning sign.
