Definition

What is GEO?

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is the discipline of structuring and writing content so that AI answer engines cite your brand as a source within their generated answers. Where SEO optimises for ranked links on a search results page, GEO optimises for inclusion in the synthesised response that an AI gives directly to the user.

When someone asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google Gemini, or Google's AI Overviews a question, the engine composes one answer from many sources. GEO makes your content one of those trusted, cited sources, the modern equivalent of ranking number one.

Why it matters in 2025+

The answer is replacing the link

Search is shifting from a list of ten links to a single, synthesised answer. That changes everything about how brands get discovered.

Zero-click is rising

More queries end without a click to any website, the answer is delivered inline. Citations, not clicks, become the visibility metric.

Models reason on entities

AI engines understand the world as entities and relationships. Clearly defining who you are, what you do, and where, makes you citable.

Early movers win

GEO is still emerging. Brands that optimise now establish themselves as default sources before their competitors notice the shift.

GEO vs SEO

Two disciplines, one foundation

SEO

Optimises for ranked links on a search results page. Success = position one. Click-through is the goal.

GEO

Optimises for citations inside AI answers. Success = being named and linked in the synthesised response. Inclusion is the goal.

The good news: the same entity-rich, well-structured, factual content foundation powers both. That is why Kay & Co. delivers SEO and GEO as a single, compounding strategy.
